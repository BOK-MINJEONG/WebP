Economic Impact

According to a PwC report, by 2030, artificial intelligence is expected to add up to $15.7 trillion to the global GDP. China and North America are expected to lead this growth, potentially increasing their GDPs by approximately 26% and 14.5% respectively through AI.

McKinsey research anticipates that about 70% of businesses will implement AI in some form by 2030.

Industry-specific Changes

Manufacturing

Enhances production efficiency.

Healthcare

AI has the potential to save the healthcare industry more than $150 billion annually. It will particularly impact diagnostics, patient management, and optimization of treatment protocols.

Automotive Industry

With the commercialization of autonomous vehicles, the global market for self-driving cars is expected to grow to over $60 billion by 2025.

Operational Improvement

Customer Self-service

Generative AI-powered chatbots can provide immediate and personalized responses to complex customer inquiries regardless of the customer's language or location. By enhancing the quality and efficiency of interactions through automated channels, generative AI can automate responses to more customer inquiries, allowing customer service teams to handle queries that only human agents can resolve. According to McKinsey, about half of all customer inquiries handled by banks, telecommunications, and utilities in North America are already processed by machines, including AI. Depending on the existing level of automation within a company, using generative AI could reduce the volume of customer inquiries handled directly by people by up to 50%.

Problem Resolution at First Contact

Generative AI can immediately access data a company holds on a specific customer, helping human customer service agents to more successfully respond to questions and resolve issues during initial interactions.

Reduction in Response Time

Generative AI can provide support in real-time and recommend next steps, reducing the time human sales agents spend engaging with customers.

Revenue Increase

This technology's ability to quickly process data about customers and their search history allows it to identify product suggestions and transactions that align with customer preferences. Additionally, generative AI can gather insights from customer conversations, find better methods, and coach agents, thereby improving quality assurance and coaching. McKinsey estimates that applying generative AI to customer management functions could increase productivity by 30-45% of current function costs.

Employment Changes

According to a World Economic Forum (WEF) report, by 2025, artificial intelligence and automation technologies are expected to displace about 85 million jobs, but at the same time, create approximately 97 million new jobs.

It is estimated that about 120 million workers will need retraining from 2022 to 2030 to adapt to AI and automation.

Ethical Considerations and Regulation

The European Union has implemented GDPR, one of the strictest regulations in the world concerning AI and data protection, and the United States and China are also strengthening regulations on the use of AI. More detailed regulations are expected to emerge in the future.
