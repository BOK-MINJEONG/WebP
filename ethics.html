Privacy and Data Ethics

Artificial intelligence systems learn from massive amounts of data, a significant portion of which includes personal information. For example, according to an IBM study in 2021, 80% of the data used by companies contained personalized information (IBM, 2021). The collection and use of such data increase the potential for privacy violations, thus necessitating appropriate data protection regulations and ethical guidelines.

Transparency in Decision-Making

The decision-making process of AI systems is often referred to as a "black box," with its internal workings being difficult to understand from the outside. For example, Facebook uses its algorithms to determine which content is shown to users, but this lack of transparency in the decision-making process led to significant criticism in 2018 (The New York Times, 2018). Such issues can undermine fair treatment of users and accountability for erroneous decisions.

Privacy and Data Security

AI's ability to collect and process vast amounts of data can increase concerns over privacy breaches, making ethical considerations essential.

Automation and Employment

The introduction of AI can replace certain jobs, creating an imbalance between job creation and job loss. The World Economic Forum's 2020 report predicted that AI and automation technologies would eliminate about 85 million jobs by 2025 but also create 97 million new jobs. This represents a significant change that will necessitate the redesign of education and training programs.

Algorithmic Bias and Discrimination

AI systems are at risk of learning biases inherent in the input data. For example, in 2019, there were claims that the Apple Card's credit limit decisions were discriminatory based on gender (BBC, 2019). Such algorithmic biases can reinforce inequalities against marginalized social groups.
